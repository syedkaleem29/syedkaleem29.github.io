About Hon-e-Ken

Department of ELECTRONICS AND COMMUNICATION ENGINEERING under the precious shadow of RMK ENGINEERING COLLEGE solefully presents HON-E-KEN 2K16 , A National Level Technical Symposium. It has been a cyclic activity of our department to organize such a fest as a grandeur one. As the title indicates, the candidates participating in this will sharpen their skills at a greater extent. We share immense pride and laurel in organizing the events more effectively. Ultimate encouragement from the management and the department head add an extra beauty to the programme. Anxious moments are awaiting you at HON-E-KEN 2K16 on 26.09.2015.

Rules

  • A team can have a minimum of 2 to a maximum of 3 members.

  • The hint for the hunt of treasure has its roots in the basics and current trends of electronics.

Description

Description of arena.

Arena will not be displayed earlier. Info regarding arena is as follows:

  • The track may consists of obstacles like:
    • Gravel with obstacles.
    • Valleys, see-saw.
    • Steps, slopes, rotating elements.
  • The time limit is a maximum of 5 minutes.
  • Width of the track is 35 cm.
  • Penalties are imposed.

Team completing the track in least time will be the winner.

Rules

  • Maximum of three members per team.
  • The BOT size should be of dimension of 25 * 25 * 25 cm.(Tolerance of +/- 3cm will be permitted).
  • BOT should not damage the arena.
  • BOT should go through all the obstacles and reach the end line.
  • Maximum of 12 V potential drop and 350 rpm on any point of the BOT.
  • External battery power supply is permitted.
  • BOT can be wired or wireless. If wired, wires should be more than 3 to 4 metres. If wireless, atleast two different frequencies should be brought.
  • Guidelines mentioned are subjected to change.
  • Judges decision will be considered final.
  • Organizers are not responsible for damage of BOT in track obstacle.

Description

BUG-O-DEBUG presents you the opportunity to bring out your technical knowledge and unshackle the creativity in you with the enthralling rounds relating to the Electronics and Communication subjects. A perfect scenario to apply practically of what you have been cramming so far. Let your debugging skills spring-up and take away the prizes.

Rules

Level 1: Priliminary Round

  • Maximum 2 members in a batch.
  • There will be 15 Questions for 20 minutes.
  • Batches will be selected for the final round depending on the score.

Level 2: Final Round

  • The bug circuit should be debugged and the output should be shown along with explanation.
  • All the components will be provided.
  • No external components are allowed.
  • If there is any damage to components, the batch will be disqualified.
  • Time taken to complete the debugging will also be considered and accordingly the prizes will be announced.
  • Decision of the judge will be final.
